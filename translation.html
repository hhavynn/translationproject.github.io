The Song of the Pipa

ChatGPT Version

At Xunyang River's head, I bid farewell in the night,
Autumn leaves and reeds rustle, a scene so right.
Master dismounts, guest boards the boat's flight,
Raises his cup, but no music for delight.

Drunk, we fail to part with merry hearts,
Departing amidst the moonlit river, vast.
Suddenly, the sound of a pipa breaks the spell,
Master forgets to return, guest remains as well.

Inquiring quietly about the musician's name,
As the pipa's sound halts, speech is tame.
Boats draw near, inviting an encounter keen,
Adding wine, relighting the lamp, the scene serene.

With countless calls, the musician finally appears,
Clutching his pipa, partly veiling his fears.
Strings plucked thrice, melody not yet formed,
Each note resonates with emotions warmed.

Strings suppressed, each sound whispers woes,
Echoing a lifetime of unfulfilled throes.
Brows low, fingers swift, melodies unfold,
Telling tales of untold struggles, never told.

Gently plucking, gradually strumming,
From the Nishang to the Liu Yao, becoming.
Thick strings sound like a downpour's grace,
Thin strings whisper secrets in a private space.

Clattering, rustling, a cacophony of sound,
Large and small pearls on a jade tray found.
Amidst the sound, nightingales murmur near,
Subdued murmurs, streams under moonlight clear.

The water's coldness, the strings freeze,
Silenced, the music temporarily at ease.
In this silence, hidden sorrows breed,
Now, silence triumphs, surpassing any need.

Silver vase shatters, water splashes high,
Iron knights emerge, swords and spears fly.
As the melody concludes, hearts are moved,
Four strings resonate, like silk, they're grooved.

East boats, west ferries, silent they glide,
Only the autumn moon gleams, a guide.
Contemplating, plucking, amidst the strings' lay,
Tidying attire, preparing to bid the day.

Admitting, once, a lady of the capital proud,
Now residing near Xiami Hill, unbowed.
At thirteen, mastered the pipa's art,
Ranked first in the imperial court's chart.

After performances, praises showered so well,
Yet adorned, Autumn Beauties envied her spell.
Youths from the Five Ridges vie for her affection,
Countless times, red silk spun, beyond perception.

Jade combs, cloud hairpins, beat in rhythm bold,
Scarlet skirts swirl, stained with wine gold.
This year's laughter, next year's mirth,
Autumn moon, spring breeze, an endless birth.

Brother joins the army, aunt passes away,
With each sunrise, sunset, colors gray.
Before the door, deserted, carriages few,
Elder sister married, a merchant's wife, true.

Merchants value profit, lightly they part,
Last month, floating beams, tea to depart.
Coming and going, at the river's mouth waits,
Moonlit river, chilly, navigates.

Deep in the night, youthful dreams revive,
Dreaming, tears mix with rouge, hearts strive.
I hear the pipa sigh with deep regret,
Echoes of sorrow, each note wet.

Both exiles, in this world's distant land,
Meeting now, need not recognize where we stand.
I, from last year, bid the capital goodbye,
Banished, to bedridden Xunyang I did comply.

Xunyang, remote, devoid of melody's grace,
Year after year, no sound of strings to trace.
Near Pian River, lowlands damp and chill,
Yellow cottages, bamboo thickets, my will.

At dawn and dusk, what do I hear,
Cuckoos crying, gibbons wailing near.
In spring, flowers bloom, autumn's moon bright,
Often taking wine, returning alone, a silent plight.

Without mountain songs or village flutes in ear,
Hoarse laughter, difficult to hear.
Tonight, hearing your pipa's gentle strains,
Like celestial music, my senses regain.

Do not refuse to play another tune,
For you, I'll pen another "Pipa Tune."
Moved by my words, you stood for a while,
But as you played, the strings' pace did compile.

Sorrowful, unlike the sounds of yore,
In the crowd, tears flowed, not just one, but more.
Whose tears fell most in this gathering's array?
Jiangzhou's magistrate, his blue robe, now gray.

Author: Bai Juyi

By Havyn Nguyen, Carmen Ren, and Sifan Wu
